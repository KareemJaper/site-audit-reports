Executive Summary

Miska Store is an e-commerce website selling perfumes and body care products, built on the Shopify platform. The overall design is clean, professional, and well-suited for a premium fragrance brand.

However, this audit identified 6 key improvement areas — the most critical being the absence of customer reviews and social proof, the hero section's offer not being prominent enough, and missing product card information.

Implementing the 3 high-priority improvements alone is expected to increase conversion rates by 15–25% based on e-commerce industry benchmarks.

User Experience (UX) Issues

4 Issues
High 1. Missing Customer Reviews & Social Proof

Product cards do not display any ratings or customer reviews. The homepage lacks a testimonials section or any form of social proof.

Impact: 93% of consumers read reviews before purchasing. The absence of ratings directly reduces trust and conversion rates.

Recommendation: Add star ratings to every product card + create a "What Our Customers Say" section on the homepage with real photos and names + add "Bought by X people this week" social proof indicators.
High 2. Hero Section — Offer Not Prominent Enough

The site does feature a promotional offer in the scrolling ticker bar below the hero, but the main banner itself ("Miska's 5th Anniversary") lacks a visible offer or a strong call-to-action button. The ticker is easy to overlook, especially on first impression.

Impact: Visitors decide within 3–5 seconds whether to continue browsing or leave. If the offer is buried in a secondary element, many users will miss it entirely — increasing bounce rates.

Recommendation: Surface the offer directly inside the hero banner with a large, contrasting CTA button (e.g., "Shop the Sale Now") so it's immediately visible without relying on the ticker alone.
High 3. Product Cards Missing Critical Information

Product cards currently display only: image + name + price. They are missing: ratings, availability status, discounts/original price, and promotional badges (bestseller, new arrival).

Impact: Insufficient information for quick purchase decisions, forcing users to click into every product page, slowing down the buying journey.

Recommendation: Enrich product cards with: (1) Star ratings (2) "Bestseller" / "New" badges (3) Stock indicator "Only 3 left" (4) Strikethrough original price when discounted.
Medium 4. No Urgency or Scarcity Indicators

There are no indicators of limited availability or time-sensitive offers. No countdown timers or "X items left" notifications are present.

Recommendation: Add an announcement bar at the top with a time-limited offer + display low-stock indicators on products nearing sell-out.

Visual Interface (UI) Issues

2 Issues
Medium 1. Product Card Font Sizes Too Small

Product names and prices on cards are relatively small, making quick scanning difficult — especially on mobile devices.

Recommendation: Increase product name to 16–18px and price to 18–20px bold. The size difference creates better visual hierarchy and improves scannability.
Low 2. Insufficient Spacing Between Sections

Some homepage sections are placed too close together, lacking adequate visual breathing room.

Recommendation: Increase section padding to at least 60–80px. Generous whitespace conveys the premium feel appropriate for the brand.
